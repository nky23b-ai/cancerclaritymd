WHAT WE OFFER

Specialist-Level Support for Complex Cases

Cancer Clarity MD provides independent, AI-assisted, physician-validated second opinions for oncology and hematology cases. Our reports are structured to complement your clinical workflow - not replace it.

Genomic & NGS Interpretation

Expert review of Next-Generation Sequencing and molecular profiling reports to identify actionable mutations, resistance mechanisms, and targeted therapy options.

Clinical Trial Matching

Systematic identification of currently enrolling trials matched to the patient's specific clinical and molecular profile, including eligibility pre-screening.

Rare & Complex Presentations

Access to sub-specialists in rare solid tumors, hematologic malignancies, and atypical presentations where standard guidelines may not fully apply.

Treatment Plan Review

Independent assessment of proposed or ongoing treatment regimens, including chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and stem cell transplant planning.

Pathology & Staging Correlation

Correlation of pathology findings with clinical staging, imaging, and biomarker data to ensure diagnostic accuracy before treatment initiation.

Benign Hematology Consultation

Specialist input on complex benign hematologic conditions including cytopenias, coagulation disorders, hemoglobinopathies, and bone marrow pathology.

THE PROCESS

Three Steps to a Specialist Report

1

Submit Referral

Complete the secure referral form below with your clinical question and patient details. No records needed at this stage.

2

Secure Record Transfer

Our clinical coordination team contacts you to arrange HIPAA-compliant transfer of pathology, imaging, labs, and relevant notes.

3

Report & Peer Discussion

A structured written opinion is delivered within 48–72 hours, followed by a direct peer-to-peer call to discuss findings and recommendations.

OUR METHODOLOGY

Clinically Evaluated. Specialist Approved.

Our workflow uses AI-assisted tools to systematically organize and cross-reference submitted records - pathology, imaging, genomics, and clinical notes - before the case reaches the reviewing specialist.

This ensures the physician receives a structured, complete case summary rather than a disorganized stack of documents, enabling a more focused and clinically precise opinion. All conclusions and recommendations are authored and validated by a board-certified specialist.

Structured Record Organization

AI tools organize submitted records chronologically and by category before physician review.

Literature & Trial Cross-Reference

Cases are cross-referenced against NCCN Guidelines, ASCO and ESMO updates, ASH presentations, and active clinical trials before reaching the reviewing physician.

Physician-Authored Conclusions

Every recommendation in the final report is written and signed by the reviewing board-certified specialist.
